El Torito

El Torito (meaning "the bull" in English) was the byproduct of me and my grandpa collaborating to make art. Originally my grandpa had made the sculpture out of bronze by hand at a small scale, but he wanted it to be much bigger. So, I worked with him and designed the sculpture using Autodesk Fusion CAD software. The CAD ended up being enough for him to submit the design to art contests, and it was chosen for the Green Bay Rotating Arts Program in 2022. With the money received from that, we got the chance to make the CAD full-scale out of metal, and showcase it in Green Bay, WI.

Smart Door Knob

Unlike other smart door locks which you might find on Amazon or elsewhere, this door knob is designed to fit a typical bedroom door (to keep siblings out of course). I designed the whole fixture in CAD to be small, lightweight, and 3D-printable, so that it could fit on a typical door inside a home. In addition, I also made a custom PCB that integrates a servo motor, buttons, and wireless communications so that the door knob can be unlocked manually with button input, or through Amazon Alexa, or even through my phone!

iPad Letterboard

The iPad Letterboard app that I made was designed to be compatible with my little brother's spelling board that he uses to spell to communicate. The app was primarily designed to be used with his actual physical letterboard (pictured above on top of the iPad), so that he could receive physical feedback whenever he was interacting with it. The app also made it so that tapping through the letterboard with a touch-screen compatible pencil would record the letter that he hit, so that the person he wants to communicate with could read what he was trying to spell.
